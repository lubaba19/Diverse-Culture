About Pakistan

Pakistan is found in Southern Asia, between India within the east, Iran and Afghanistan within the west, China within the north and bordering the Middle eastern Ocean within the south. Pakistan is not a multicltural country. It has it's own distinct culture.

Famous food

There are lots of famous food in Pakistan. Each type of food dish have it's own famous food. Like In Meat Dish "Sajji", in Dessert "Zarda", in Porridge "Haleem", in Lamb/Mutton Dish "Seekh kabab", in Stew "Nihari", in ground Meat Dish "Chapli kabab", in Chicken Dish "Chicken karahi" and "Chicken biryani", and in street Food "Chaat" is the famous food.

Famous clothes

In 1973, shalwar kameez became the national dress of Pakistan. It is worn by men and women in all provinces (Punjab, Sindh, Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa And Gilgit-Baltistan) of the country and in Azad Kashmir. Shalwar refers to loose trousers and kameez refers to shirts. For the parties and weddings, men wear kurta, Pakistani Waistcoat, churidar or pajama. Headgear includes the Jinnah Cap also called Karakul, Fez also called Rumi Topi and Taqiyah (cap), and The most popular Pakistani bridal dress Women wears is the sharara-kurta. It consists of a long fitted shirt or kurta and a pair of ruched wide-legged pants, called the sharara. This is combined with a long veil that is wrapped over the head and/or around the shoulders. Peshawari chappal and Khussa are popular foot wear.

National Language

Urdu is the national language of Pakistan. It is a mixture of Persian, Arabic and various local languages. Urdu was chosen as an official language of Pakistan in 1947 as it was already the lingua franca for Muslims in north and northwest British India, although Urdu had been used as a literary medium for colonial Indian writers from the Bombay Presidency, Bengal, Orissa Province, and Tamil Nadu as well.

Famous festival

There are lots of festivals to be celebrated in Pakistan. Such as Eid-ul-Fitr (a gift from God in return of fasting for 30 days), Eid-ul-adha, Chaand raat (a night before Eid-ul-Fitr), Quaid-e-Azam Day (The birthday of a founder of Pakistan), Independence day of Pakistan, flower show and many more festivals.
